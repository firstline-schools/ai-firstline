Gmail

Where most FirstLine folks spend the most time. Two tricks that hand it back to you.

FLS Essentials

Schedule Send — draft late, deliver at the right hour

Draft a parent email at 10pm; deliver it at 7am. Preserves your evening without slowing your work.

How to do it
  1. Compose your email.
  2. Click the arrow next to Send.
  3. Pick Schedule send → date/time.
FLS note: Aligns with our "disciplined" principle and sets healthy expectations about response times.

"Offer times you're free" — instant meeting booking

Drop clickable time blocks into an email. Recipients pick one; both calendars book automatically.

How to do it
  1. Open a draft or reply.
  2. Click the Calendar icon in the toolbar.
  3. Choose "Offer times you're free" and drag your open slots.
  4. Click Insert into email.
FLS note: Great for parent-teacher meeting rounds. Cuts 5–10 back-and-forth emails per request.

Google Docs

Where a lot of your day happens. Small tricks compound fast.

FLS Essentials

The @ menu — Docs' universal insert shortcut

Type @ anywhere for instant access to tables, dropdowns, meeting notes, colleague chips, and files.

Try these
  • • @table — insert a table
  • • @meeting — auto-fill from Calendar event
  • • @dropdown — status trackers
  • • @name — insert a people chip
FLS note: Once you use @meeting, you never go back to hand-formatting minutes.

Voice typing — dictate observations and meeting notes

Capture classroom observation notes on the walk back to your desk. Google's voice recognition is very accurate and understands punctuation commands.

How to do it
  1. Tools → Voice typing (⌘+Shift+S on Mac).
  2. Click the microphone → speak naturally.
  3. Say "period," "comma," or "new paragraph" for punctuation.
FLS note: Especially useful for post-observation notes and IEP session recaps.

Google Sheets

The engine behind anything with structured data. These two hit hardest for FirstLine.

FLS Essentials

GOOGLETRANSLATE() — bulk-translate family communications

Write a message column in English; render it in Spanish, Vietnamese, or Arabic in a single formula.

=GOOGLETRANSLATE(A2, "en", "es")

Codes: es, vi, ar, fr. Use "auto" to auto-detect source.

FLS note: Directly serves multilingual family engagement.

Dot notation — extract emails and names from Smart Chips

When a cell holds a Smart Chip (person, file, event), pull properties off it like an object.

=A2.email

Also: .name, .[file name], .attendees.

FLS note: Advanced but transforms how you manage rosters.

Google Vids

Workspace's newest tool — turn a script into a video with AI-generated scenes, stock media, and narration. Great for parent updates, staff PD, and asynchronous content.

FLS Essentials

Draft a video from a Google Doc or outline

Paste a lesson script, announcement, or outline into Vids. It generates matching scenes with stock media, transitions, and captions. Edit from there rather than starting blank.

How to do it
  1. Open vids.google.com.
  2. Click + Create video → paste your script or outline.
  3. Use Help me create to generate scenes automatically.
  4. Refine scene-by-scene; the AI draft is a starting point.
FLS note: Great for weekly parent updates, staff PD explainers, and family-facing videos. Do not include student-identifying information in scripts.

AI voice narration — no recording setup

Type the script, pick an AI-generated voice, get instant narration. Multiple accents and languages available — useful when you don't have time (or a quiet room) to record yourself.

How to do it
  1. Select a scene in Vids.
  2. Click Voiceover → Generate with AI.
  3. Type or paste script → pick a voice → preview → apply.
FLS note: Record your own voice for anything student-facing that benefits from your relationship with them. AI voice is fine for logistics, PD, and family updates.

AI Prompts

Prompts tested against FirstLine work. Human review required for anything that becomes an artifact.

FLS Essentials

IEP progress note drafter — strengths-based language

Feed observation notes to Gemini; get IEP-appropriate first drafts. Saves the "how do I phrase this?" work.

"You are helping a special education teacher draft an IEP progress note. Given these observations: [paste notes], write a progress summary in professional, strengths-based language covering: current performance, progress toward the goal, and next steps. Do not fabricate data."
FLS note: FirstLine-original. Human review required. Do not paste student-identifying information into consumer AI — use Gemini Enterprise.

Reading-level translator — differentiate any text

Paste dense text; get an accessible version at the reading level you need. Preserves core vocabulary.

"Rewrite this text at a 3rd-grade reading level. Maintain the key vocabulary words but shorten sentence structures."
FLS note: Approved for Gemini Enterprise. Do not paste student-identifying information.
