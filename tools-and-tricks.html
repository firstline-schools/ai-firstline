Gmail Tip #001

Schedule Send — draft late, deliver at the right hour

▸

Draft parent emails whenever you have the bandwidth; deliver at 7am when they'll actually read them. Preserves your evening without slowing your work.

How to do it:

  1. Open Gmail and compose your email.
  2. Click the small arrow next to the blue Send button.
  3. Choose Schedule send — pick a suggested time or set your own.
  4. Click Schedule send to confirm.
Contributor: Scott Shirey · Aligns with our "disciplined" principle. Sending parent-facing email at professional hours sets a healthy expectation about response times.
Docs Tip #002

The @ menu — Docs' universal insert shortcut

▸

Type @ anywhere in a Google Doc to insert tables, dropdowns, meeting notes, colleague chips, or files. Skip nested menus forever.

How to do it:

  1. Open any Google Doc.
  2. Click in the body, type @ (just the @ symbol, no space).
  3. A menu appears with categories: People, Files, Building blocks, Meeting notes, and more.
  4. Type to filter, or use arrow keys to navigate.
  5. Press Enter to insert.

Try these first:

  • • @table — insert a table
  • • @meeting — auto-fill from a Calendar event
  • • @dropdown — status/tracker fields
  • • @colleague-name — insert a smart people chip
Contributor: Scott Shirey · Once you use @meeting once, you never go back to hand-formatting minutes.
Docs Tip #003

Voice typing — dictate observation notes

▸

Capture classroom observation notes on the walk back to your desk. Google's voice recognition is very accurate and understands punctuation commands.

How to do it:

  1. Open a Google Doc in Chrome (voice typing only works in Chrome).
  2. Tools menu → Voice typing (shortcut: ⌘+Shift+S on Mac, Ctrl+Shift+S on Windows).
  3. A microphone icon appears in the left margin. Click it.
  4. Allow microphone access when Chrome asks (first time only).
  5. Speak naturally. Say "period," "comma," "question mark," or "new paragraph" for punctuation.
  6. Click the mic again to stop.
Contributor: Scott Shirey · Especially useful for post-observation notes and IEP session recaps. If disabled, check Chrome/OS mic permission or ask Joe (IT) to enable in Workspace admin.
Sheets Tip #004

GOOGLETRANSLATE() — bulk-translate family communications

▸

Write outreach messages in English; render them in Spanish, Vietnamese, Arabic — whatever your community needs — in one formula. Removes the translation bottleneck from family communications.

How to do it:

  1. Open a Google Sheet.
  2. Put your English text in column A (starting at A2).
  3. In cell B2, type the formula below and press Enter.
  4. Drag the formula down column B to translate the entire column.
=GOOGLETRANSLATE(A2, "en", "es")

Language codes: es (Spanish), vi (Vietnamese), ar (Arabic), fr (French), ht (Haitian Creole). Use "auto" as the source to auto-detect.

Contributor: Scott Shirey · Directly serves multilingual family engagement. For a whole document (permission slip, letter), see Tip #008.
AI Prompt Tip #005

Reading-level translator — differentiate any text

▸

Paste dense text into Gemini Enterprise; get an accessible version at the grade band you need. Preserves core vocabulary but restructures sentences.

How to do it:

  1. Open Gemini Enterprise.
  2. Paste the source text you want to differentiate.
  3. Add the prompt below (edit the grade level as needed).
  4. Review the output. Edit for accuracy before use.
"Rewrite this text at a 3rd-grade reading level. Maintain the key vocabulary words but shorten sentence structures."
Contributor: Scott Shirey · Approved for Gemini Enterprise. Do not paste student-identifying information.
AI Prompt Tip #006

IEP progress note drafter — strengths-based language

▸

Feed observation notes to Gemini; get IEP-appropriate first drafts in strengths-based language. Saves the "how do I phrase this?" work and gets teachers back to instruction.

How to do it:

  1. Open Gemini Enterprise.
  2. Paste the prompt below.
  3. Replace [paste notes] with your actual observation notes.
  4. Review the output. Human edit is required before finalizing.
"You are helping a special education teacher draft an IEP progress note. Given these observations: [paste notes], write a progress summary in professional, strengths-based language covering: current performance, progress toward the goal, and next steps. Do not fabricate data."
Contributor: Scott Shirey · FirstLine-original. Human review required — you own the accuracy. Do not paste student-identifying information into consumer AI tools — use Gemini Enterprise only.
Meet Tip #007

Live-translate parent conferences in Google Meet

▸

Google Meet's Gemini-powered Live Translate does real-time speech translation during a call. Perfect for parent conferences when a family speaks Spanish, Vietnamese, Arabic — or any other language — and you don't. You speak English; the parent hears their language. They speak; you see subtitles in English. No interpreter required.

How to use it:

  1. Start a Google Meet call as normal.
  2. Open the settings (bottom bar) and turn on Captions.
  3. Choose Translated captions and select the family's language.
  4. Speak naturally — the family sees their language in real time (see video above for the exact interface).
Contributor: Scott Shirey · Requires a Workspace edition that includes Meet AI features. Test before the call to confirm it's enabled on your account. Especially strong for FLS's multilingual family engagement.
Docs Tip #008

Translate an entire Google Doc in one click

▸

For anything longer than a message column (permission slips, family letters, syllabus, staff handbook sections), Docs will translate the whole document at once. Formatting is preserved. Original stays intact — Docs creates a new translated copy.

How to do it:

  1. Open the Doc you want to translate.
  2. Tools menu → Translate document.
  3. Name the new file (e.g., "Family Letter — Spanish") and pick the target language.
  4. Click Translate. A new tab opens with the translated copy in your Drive.
Contributor: Scott Shirey · Three ways to translate at FLS: Docs → whole document (this tip), Sheets → GOOGLETRANSLATE() per cell (Tip #004), Gemini Enterprise → paste any text and ask. Pick the one that fits your source format.
Vids Tip #009

Turn a Google Slides deck into a shareable video

▸

Already made the deck? Vids imports it, layers in narration, and exports a video — no starting from scratch. Great for turning PD decks into on-demand videos, parent-night presentations into follow-up content families can rewatch, or lesson slides into flipped-classroom videos.

How to do it:

  1. Open vids.google.com.
  2. Create a new video → choose Import from Google Slides.
  3. Pick your deck. Each slide becomes a scene.
  4. Add narration — record your own voice, or use AI voice (see Tip #021 in the master library for the AI narration workflow).
  5. Adjust scene timing, add transitions, drop in stock media if needed.
  6. Click Share → get a link, or Download as MP4.
Contributor: Scott Shirey · Repurpose anything you've already made. Combined with Tip #007 (Meet Live Translate), your videos reach families in any language. Do not include student-identifying information in scripts.
