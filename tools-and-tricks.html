Gmail Tip #001

Schedule Send — draft late, deliver at the right hour

Draft parent emails whenever you have the bandwidth; deliver at 7am when they'll actually read them. Compose → arrow next to Send → Schedule send.

Contributor: Scott Shirey · Aligns with our "disciplined" principle.
Docs Tip #002

The @ menu — Docs' universal insert shortcut

Type @ anywhere in a Doc to insert tables, dropdowns, meeting notes, or people chips. Try @meeting and pick a Calendar event — attendees + notes template appear automatically.

Contributor: Scott Shirey · Once you use @meeting, you never go back.
Docs Tip #003

Voice typing — dictate observation notes

Capture classroom observation notes on the walk back to your desk. Tools → Voice typing (⌘+Shift+S on Mac). Say "period," "comma," or "new paragraph" for punctuation.

Contributor: Scott Shirey · Especially useful for post-observation notes.
Sheets Tip #004

GOOGLETRANSLATE() — bulk-translate family communications

Write outreach messages in English; render them in Spanish, Vietnamese, or Arabic in one formula:

=GOOGLETRANSLATE(A2, "en", "es")
Contributor: Scott Shirey · Directly serves multilingual family engagement.
AI Prompt Tip #005

Reading-level translator — differentiate any text

Paste dense text into Gemini Enterprise; get a version at the grade band you need. Preserves core vocabulary.

"Rewrite this text at a 3rd-grade reading level. Maintain the key vocabulary words but shorten sentence structures."
Contributor: Scott Shirey · Approved for Gemini Enterprise. Do not paste student-identifying information.
AI Prompt Tip #006

IEP progress note drafter — strengths-based language

Feed observation notes to Gemini; get IEP-appropriate first drafts. Saves the "how do I phrase this?" work.

"You are helping a special education teacher draft an IEP progress note. Given these observations: [paste notes], write a progress summary in professional, strengths-based language covering: current performance, progress toward the goal, and next steps. Do not fabricate data."
Contributor: Scott Shirey · Human review always required. You own the accuracy.
