Gmail

Gmail Easy ⏱ Saves 3–5 min per meeting

"Offer times you're free" — instant meeting booking

Instead of the "does 2pm work? How about 3?" back-and-forth, drop clickable time blocks straight into your email. Recipients pick one and it books both calendars automatically.

How to do it

  1. Open a draft or reply in Gmail.
  2. Click the Calendar icon in the bottom formatting toolbar.
  3. Choose "Offer times you're free" and drag your open slots in the side panel.
  4. Click Insert into email.
Gmail Easy NEW ⏱ Protects your evenings

Schedule Send — draft late, deliver at the right hour

Draft a parent email at 10pm without arriving at 10pm. Schedule it to land at 7am when parents actually read email — and you don't set the expectation that you're always available.

How to do it

  1. Compose your email.
  2. Click the arrow next to Send.
  3. Pick Schedule send → choose a suggested time or set your own.

Google Docs

Docs Easy ⏱ Saves countless clicks

The @ menu — Docs' universal insert shortcut

The @ symbol is Docs' universal insert menu. Type it anywhere to bring up files, tables, meeting notes, dropdowns, and colleague chips — no more digging through nested menus.

Try these

  • •  @table — insert a table
  • •  @dropdown — status/tracker fields
  • •  @meeting — auto-populate from a Calendar event
  • •  @colleague-name — insert a smart people chip
  • •  @email draft — collaborative email drafting → push to Gmail
Docs Easy NEW ⏱ 3–5× faster than typing

Voice typing — dictate observations and meeting notes

Great for capturing classroom observation notes on the walk back to your desk, or draining a meeting recap into a Doc without typing. Google's voice recognition is very accurate and understands punctuation commands.

How to do it

  1. Tools menu → Voice typing (or ⌘+Shift+S on Mac).
  2. Click the microphone → speak naturally.
  3. Say "period," "comma," or "new paragraph" for punctuation.

Google Sheets

Sheets Intermediate ⏱ Eliminates copy-paste

Extract emails, names & metadata with dot notation

When a Sheets cell holds a Smart Chip (a person, a Doc, a Calendar event), you can pull properties off it like an object. Works for names, emails, file titles, event attendees, and last-modified timestamps.

=A2.email

Also try: =A2.name, =A2.[file name], =A2.[last modified time], =A2.attendees.

Sheets Intermediate NEW ⏱ Family comms at scale

GOOGLETRANSLATE() — bulk-translate family communications

Draft a message column in English, translate the whole column to Spanish, Vietnamese, or Arabic in a single formula. Perfect for outreach lists, permission slips, and signage where translation would otherwise be a bottleneck.

=GOOGLETRANSLATE(A2, "en", "es")

Second argument = source language, third = target. Use "auto" to auto-detect. Common codes: es (Spanish), vi (Vietnamese), ar (Arabic), fr (French).

AI Prompts

AI Prompt Easy ⏱ 15+ min saved per text

Reading-level translator — differentiate any text in seconds

Paste any dense text and get an accessible version at the reading level you need. Preserves core vocabulary but restructures sentences for the target grade band. Works in Gemini, Claude, or ChatGPT.

"Rewrite this text at a 3rd-grade reading level. Maintain the key vocabulary words but shorten sentence structures."

Swap "3rd-grade" for your target level. Great for adapting news articles, textbook passages, or district letters.

AI Prompt Intermediate NEW ⏱ 20+ min per IEP note

IEP progress note drafter — strengths-based language, fast

Feed raw observation notes to Gemini and get an IEP-appropriate first draft. Human review always required (per FLS policy) — but this saves the "how do I phrase this?" work and gets teachers back to instruction.

"You are helping a special education teacher draft an IEP progress note. Given these observations: [paste notes], write a progress summary in professional, strengths-based language covering: current performance, progress toward the goal, and next steps. Do not fabricate data."
Reminder: Always review, verify, and edit before finalizing. This drafts language — you own the accuracy.
