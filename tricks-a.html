Tricks

  • Instead of the "does 2pm work? How about 3?" back-and-forth, drop clickable time blocks straight into your email. Recipients pick one and it books both calendars automatically.

    How to do it:

    1. Open a draft or reply in Gmail.
    2. Click the Calendar icon in the bottom formatting toolbar.
    3. Choose "Offer times you're free" and drag your open slots in the side panel.
    4. Click Insert into email — done.

  • Draft a parent email at 10pm without arriving at 10pm. Schedule it for 7am when parents actually read it — and you don't set the expectation that you're always available.

    How to do it:

    1. Compose your email.
    2. Click the arrow next to Send.
    3. Pick Schedule send → choose a suggested time or set your own.

  • The @ symbol is Docs' universal insert menu. Type it to bring up files, tables, meeting notes, dropdowns, and colleague chips — no more digging through nested menus.

    Try these:

    • @table — insert a table
    • @dropdown — status/tracker fields
    • @meeting — auto-populate from a Calendar event
    • @colleague-name — insert a smart people chip

  • Great for capturing classroom observation notes on the walk back to your desk, or draining an interview into a Doc without typing. Google's voice recognition is very accurate and understands punctuation.

    How to do it:

    1. Tools menu → Voice typing (or ⌘+Shift+S on Mac).
    2. Click the microphone → speak naturally.
    3. Say "period," "comma," or "new paragraph" for punctuation.

  • When a Sheets cell holds a Smart Chip (a person, a Doc, a Calendar event), you can pull properties off it like an object. Works for names, emails, file titles, event attendees, and last-modified times.

    =A2.email

    Also try =A2.name, =A2.[file name], or =A2.attendees.

  • Draft a message column in English, translate the whole column to Spanish, Vietnamese, or Arabic in a single formula. Great for outreach, permission slips, and family-facing signage.

    =GOOGLETRANSLATE(A2, "en", "es")

    Second argument = source language, third = target. Use "auto" for auto-detect. Language codes: es (Spanish), vi (Vietnamese), ar (Arabic), fr (French).

  • Paste any dense text and get an accessible version at the reading level you need. Preserves core vocabulary but restructures sentences. Works in Gemini, Claude, or ChatGPT.

    "Rewrite this text at a 3rd-grade reading level. Maintain the key vocabulary words but shorten sentence structures."

  • Feed raw observation notes to Gemini and get an IEP-appropriate first draft. Human review always required (per FLS policy) — but this saves the "how do I phrase this?" work.

    "You are helping a special education teacher draft an IEP progress note. Given these observations: [paste notes], write a progress summary in professional, strengths-based language covering: current performance, progress toward the goal, and next steps. Do not fabricate data."

    ⚠ Always review, verify, and edit before finalizing. This drafts language — you own the accuracy.
