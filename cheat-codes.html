Cheat Codes

21 high-efficiency shortcuts, Google integrations, and prompt templates to win back your prep time.

✉️ Gmail Hacks

Insert interactive, clickable schedule blocks directly into an email reply. Recipients click a block to book instantly on both calendars.

How to do it:

  1. Open a draft or reply in Gmail.
  2. Click the Calendar icon on the bottom formatting toolbar.
  3. Select "Offer times you're free" and click-and-drag your open slots in the right-side panel.
  4. Click Insert into email.

Insert a link to your permanent booking slots (like ongoing office hours or parent-teacher conference schedules).

How to do it:

  1. Click Compose or reply in Gmail.
  2. Click the Calendar icon at the bottom of the email.
  3. Select "Link booking page" and choose your configured appointment schedule.
📄 Google Docs Magic

Stop navigating nested menu bars. Typing a single character lets you insert files, meeting notes, tables, and media instantly.

How to do it:

Type @ anywhere in Google Docs, followed by what you need (e.g., @table, @pdf, or a colleague's name) and press Enter.

Embed clean, custom status fields for task lists, grading criteria, or unit lesson pacing.

How to do it:

  1. Type @dropdown and press Enter.
  2. Choose a preset template (like Project Status) or select "New dropdown" to build custom colored options (e.g., "Not Started", "Drafting", "Submitted").

Reference coworkers or students. Hovering over their chip lets you directly email, chat, or schedule a calendar invite from inside your document canvas.

How to do it:

Type @ followed by any email address or staff name. Select their profile from the pop-up list.

Generate a comprehensive meeting minutes document that automatically imports title, date, and guest lists from your Google Calendar events.

How to do it:

  1. Type @meeting in your Doc.
  2. Select the scheduled event from the drop-down menu.

Draft sensitive communications with teammates or administrators inside a document, then launch it to Gmail with one tap.

How to do it:

  1. Type @email draft and press Enter.
  2. Collaborate with coworkers on the text inside the structured email container.
  3. Click the blue Gmail button on the left margin to push it to your drafts instantly.

Avoid navigating between multiple browser tabs to assign a finished worksheet or syllabus to your classes.

How to do it:

  1. Inside your open Google Doc, click File in the top menu.
  2. Hover over Share and select Post to Google Classroom.
  3. Configure your assignments, dates, and selections from the side-bar interface.
📅 Google Calendar Tricks

Instantly draft updates, agendas, or schedules to all event attendees without copying single email addresses.

How to do it:

  1. Open the event card inside Google Calendar.
  2. Find the guest roster on the right margin.
  3. Click the Envelope icon ("Email guests") to initiate a pre-populated email template.
📊 Google Sheets Smart Data

Extract individual contact emails directly from active People Smart Chips in your rosters.

How to do it:

In an empty cell next to your Smart Chip (e.g., cell A2), write the following and press Enter:

=A2.email

Automatically isolate the owner email or file titles from interactive Google Doc or Slide chips.

How to do it:

Reference the file chip's cell using dot-notation:

=A2.[file name]

Audit exactly when digital student work was last revised to easily monitor pacing and progress.

How to do it:

Pull the dynamic timestamp directly from the shared portfolio chip:

=A2.[last modified time]

Collate entire attendee lists directly from Calendar Event Chips to clean up your logs and conference rosters.

How to do it:

Reference the event chip's cell using dot-notation:

=A2.attendees
🗂️ Tasks, Keep & Slides (Integrations)

Save complex email directions directly into your task logs and map them straight onto your calendar.

How to do it:

  1. Open an email in Gmail.
  2. Click the Add to Tasks icon (checkmark and plus sign) in the top menu bar.
  3. This populates your sidebar list and embeds a direct, clickable link back to the source email.

Turn multi-step emails from coordinators or administrators into active checklist logs directly alongside your work screen.

How to do it:

  1. Open the email in Gmail.
  2. Click the yellow Keep icon on the right side panel and click "+ Take a note."
  3. Click the three-dot menu icon on the saved item and select "Show checkboxes."

Save prep time by trimming away noisy background elements from illustrations or clipart directly in your deck.

How to do it:

  1. Select the image on your active slide.
  2. Click the Gemini panel in the upper toolbar.
  3. Select "Remove Background" to cleanly isolate the main subject.

Brainstorm analogies designed to ground abstract learning objectives in everyday student frameworks.

Draft actionable, encouraging feedback on student rubrics quickly while keeping the tone supportive.

"Provide 3 points of actionable and positive feedback based on this student criteria list. Focus on helping them revise their [Focus Element]."

How to do it:
