Gmail

Where most FirstLine folks spend the most time. Two tricks that hand it back to you.

FLS Essentials

Schedule Send — draft late, deliver at the right hour

Draft a parent email at 10pm; deliver it at 7am. Preserves your evening without slowing your work.

How to do it
  1. Compose your email.
  2. Click the arrow next to Send.
  3. Pick Schedule send → date/time.
FLS note: Aligns with our "disciplined" principle. Sending parent-facing email at professional hours also sets a healthy expectation about response times.

"Offer times you're free" — instant meeting booking

Drop clickable time blocks into an email. Recipients pick one and it books both calendars automatically.

How to do it
  1. Open a draft or reply.
  2. Click the Calendar icon in the toolbar.
  3. Choose "Offer times you're free" and drag your open slots.
  4. Click Insert into email.
FLS note: Great for parent-teacher meeting rounds. Cuts 5–10 back-and-forth emails per meeting request.

Google Docs

Where a lot of your day happens. Small tricks compound fast.

FLS Essentials

The @ menu — Docs' universal insert shortcut

Type @ anywhere for instant access to tables, dropdowns, meeting notes, colleague chips, and files. Skip the nested menus forever.

Try these
  • •  @table — insert a table
  • •  @meeting — auto-fill from Calendar event
  • •  @dropdown — status/tracker fields
  • •  @name — insert a people chip
FLS note: Once you use @meeting, you never go back to hand-formatting minutes.

Voice typing — dictate observations and meeting notes

Capture classroom observation notes on the walk back to your desk. Google's voice recognition is very accurate and understands punctuation commands.

How to do it
  1. Tools → Voice typing (⌘+Shift+S on Mac).
  2. Click the microphone → speak naturally.
  3. Say "period," "comma," or "new paragraph" for punctuation.
FLS note: Especially useful for post-observation notes and IEP session recaps.

Google Sheets

The engine behind anything with structured data. These two hit hardest for FirstLine.

FLS Essentials

GOOGLETRANSLATE() — bulk-translate family communications

Write a message column in English; render it in Spanish, Vietnamese, or Arabic in a single formula. Removes the translation bottleneck from family communications.

=GOOGLETRANSLATE(A2, "en", "es")

Codes: es (Spanish), vi (Vietnamese), ar (Arabic), fr (French). Use "auto" to auto-detect source.

FLS note: Directly serves multilingual family engagement. Try this before hand-translating outreach lists.

Dot notation — extract emails and names from Smart Chips

When a cell holds a Smart Chip (person, file, event), pull properties off it like an object. Works for names, emails, file titles, event attendees, last-modified timestamps.

=A2.email

Also: .name, .[file name], .attendees.

FLS note: Advanced but transforms how you manage rosters, groupings, and any list that used to require copy-paste.

AI Prompts

Prompts tested against FirstLine work. Human review is required for anything that becomes an artifact.

FLS Essentials

IEP progress note drafter — strengths-based language, fast

Feed observation notes to Gemini; get IEP-appropriate first drafts. Human review always required — but this saves the "how do I phrase this?" work.

"You are helping a special education teacher draft an IEP progress note. Given these observations: [paste notes], write a progress summary in professional, strengths-based language covering: current performance, progress toward the goal, and next steps. Do not fabricate data."
FLS note: FirstLine-original prompt. You own the accuracy. Do not paste student-identifying information into consumer AI tools — use Gemini Enterprise.

Reading-level translator — differentiate any text

Paste dense text; get an accessible version at the reading level you need. Preserves core vocabulary. Works in Gemini, Claude, or ChatGPT.

"Rewrite this text at a 3rd-grade reading level. Maintain the key vocabulary words but shorten sentence structures."
FLS note: Approved for Gemini Enterprise. Swap "3rd-grade" for your target level. Do not paste student-identifying information.
