Vol. 1 · Issue 01 · May 2026 A record of how we're approaching AI at FirstLine
From the Editor

We're learning to use AI well at FirstLine. Carefully. In the open.

In April, we convened a Task Force to think carefully about AI at FirstLine. Sixteen people — teachers, leaders, parents, network staff — sat down to take on four questions, one at a time. I joined them in May as Chief Innovation Officer.

This page is where we share what we're working on, what we're learning, and what we change as a result. We won't manufacture activity to look busier. We won't celebrate before there's something to celebrate. And we'll publish the things that don't work, too.

— Scott Shirey
Chief Innovation Officer · FirstLine Schools

Section 03 · Approved this month

One Tool, On Purpose

We add tools deliberately, one at a time, when there's a clear reason. Tool decisions sit with the CIO.

G
Approved · May 2026

Gemini Enterprise

Google Cloud · No-code AI agent platform

For staff and admin use. Drafting emails, summarizing documents, brainstorming, conducting research, automating repetitive tasks. Connects securely to Google Workspace.

Why this one

FLS is already on GCP; no-code means everyone can use it.

Audience

Staff and admin first. Teacher use cases as they emerge.

Section 04 · Open call

Ten Mini-Grants. $1,000 Each. This Year.

For FirstLine staff who want to try something specific with AI — a tool, training, materials, time. Two paragraphs to apply. Fast review. The terms are simple: try the thing, write up what you learned, share it with the network.

Each grantee's findings will be published here, in future issues — what worked, what didn't, what they'd do differently.
